Troubleshooting

If audio sounds echoey, robotic, or otherwise bugged after Gain opens or activates, refresh the page or reset the extension. This should clear it up immediately.

⚠️ Audio Safety Warning

Audio levels above 250% are not recommended for regular use.

Prolonged exposure to high volume levels can cause permanent hearing damage, tinnitus (ringing in the ears), hyperacusis, or other auditory conditions. Use boosted volumes with caution and keep listening sessions short.

Audio above 250% may also cause distortion or clipping depending on the source quality and your output device.

Gain
Version 1.0.1 · Firefox Extension
What it does
Gain uses the Web Audio API to route your browser's audio through a gain node, allowing volume control from 0% to 600% — far beyond the browser's built-in limit. Bass Boost and Voice Boost apply equalizer filters to enhance your listening experience.
Permissions explained
Gain uses temporary access when you click the toolbar on a tab, and it requests persistent Firefox access only for sites you explicitly whitelist. That keeps per-site auto-start optional instead of requesting blanket access to every website.
🔒 Privacy Policy

This extension collects no personal data.

It does not track your browsing, send any information to external servers, or share anything with third parties.

All settings (blacklist, whitelist, volume preferences) are stored locally on your device using Firefox's built-in storage API and never leave your browser.

This extension was originally made for personal use, but is now public and open source after considering that there are many others probably looking for the same thing.

Cheers to an open source, privacy-focused internet.
